Cottage Pie

Description

Comfort food doesn't get much more classic than a hearty cottage pie, layered with rich beef mince and buttery mash.

This great-value family favourite freezes beautifully and is a guaranteed crowd-pleaser.

Ingredients

For the meat

  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 500g beef mince
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 carrots, chopped
  • a small bowl of garden peas
  • 1 garlic clove, finely chopped
  • 1 tbsp plain flour
  • 1 tbsp tomato purée
  • 400ml beef stock
  • 2 tbsp Worcestershire sauce
  • a few thyme sprigs

For the mash

  • 750g potatoes, chopped
  • 10g butter
  • 100g strong cheddar, grated

Steps

  1. Heat 1 tbsp olive oil in a large saucepan and fry 500g beef mince until browned, then set aside.

  2. Put the other 1 tbsp of olive oil into the pan, add 1 finely chopped onion and 2 chopped carrots, and cook on a gentle heat until soft, about 15 mins.

  3. Add 1 finely chopped garlic clove, 1 tbsp plain flour and 1 tbsp tomato purée, increase the heat and cook for a few mins, then return the beef to the pan.

  4. Adding 400ml of beef stock, 2 tbsp Worcestershire sauce, a few thyme sprigs and the small bowl of garden peas.

  5. Bring to a simmer and cook, uncovered, for 45 mins. By this time the gravy should be thick and coating the meat. Check after about 30 mins, if a lot of liquid remains, increase the heat slightly to reduce the gravy a little. Season well, then discard the thyme stalks.

  6. Meanwhile, make the mash. In a large saucepan, cover the 750g potatoes which you've peeled and chopped, in salted cold water, bring to the boil and simmer until tender.

  7. Drain well, then allow to steam-dry for a few mins. Mash well with the 10g butter, and three-quarters of the 100g strong cheddar cheese, then season with some salt and pepper.

  8. Spoon the meat into an ovenproof dish. Pipe or spoon on the mash to cover. Sprinkle on the remaining cheese.

  9. Heat the oven to 220C/200C fan/gas 7 and cook for 25-30 mins, or until the topping is golden.
